CV Research Teaching

I aim to make macroeconomics clear, structured, and relevant. I focus on economic intuition, step-by-step derivations, and a classroom where students feel comfortable asking questions.

Instructor

ECON 3302: Intermediate Macroeconomics, Southern Methodist University, Summer II 2024.

In my intermediate macroeconomics course, I revisited core mathematical tools such as derivatives to build a common foundation, wrote out derivations in detail using a tablet projected in class, and shared slides and typed lecture notes after each meeting. I held extended office hours and used current policy debates—such as recent Federal Reserve decisions and government spending programs—to connect the models to real-world questions students care about.
